Pyramids of Giza

Egypt is home to the Great Pyramids, the Pyramid of Khafre, the Pyramid of Menkaure, and the Great Sphinx. All Pyramids were built during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom of ancient Egypt, between 2600 and 2500 BC.

Valley of the Kings

The famous archaeological site in Luxor, Egypt, where many pharaohs of the New Kingdom were buried in elaborate tombs. It is best known as the resting place of Tutankhamun.

Nile River

The world's longest river, running for a length of 4,132 miles northward to the Mediterranean Sea. It is located in Egypt and has played a vital role in the country's history.

Sharm el-Sheikh

Sharm el-Sheikh is one of the main cities of the Red Sea coast and treasures a rich fauna of colorful fish and impressive corals. And one of the great places of Egypt’s coastal tourism.

Khan Al Khalili

The most famous open-air market in the Middle East. It was built in the 14th century and remains one of the oldest and largest markets, known for its vibrant atmosphere and historic charm. .

Where is Egypt?

Egypt is located in the northeastern corner of Africa. It borders the Mediterranean Sea to the north, the Gaza Strip to the northeast, the Red Sea to the east, Sudan to the south, and Libya to the west. The country is dominated by the Nile River, which flows from south to north through the eastern part of the country.

Cairo, often called "Mother of the World" (Umm al-Dunya), is Egypt's capital and largest city. This nickname dates back to the 10th century, reflecting Cairo's historical importance as a center of culture, education, and power. Today, Cairo remains the heart of Egyptian civilization, blending ancient history with modern metropolitan life.



Egyptian Historical Timeline

Period Years Notable Features
Predynastic Period c. 5500-3100 BCE Formation of kingdoms and early cultural development
Early Dynastic Period c. 3100-2686 BCE Unification of Upper and Lower Egypt
Old Kingdom c. 2686-2181 BCE Age of pyramid building, including the Great Pyramid of Giza
Middle Kingdom c. 2055-1650 BCE Revival of art, literature and architecture
New Kingdom c. 1550-1069 BCE Egypt's imperial phase, including rule of Tutankhamun
